📖 Recent Terms

Linus's Law

100/100

The idea that 'given enough eyeballs, all bugs are shallow' — meaning open collaboration makes problems easier to find and fix.

😂 "Like having a hundred proofreaders for your essay — someone will catch that typo."

open-sourcecollaborationquality

Maintainer Burnout

100/100

The exhaustion and disengagement experienced by open source maintainers who face constant demands, limited support, and little recognition.

😂 "Like being the only bartender at a never-ending party where everyone wants a custom cocktail, but nobody tips."

communitysustainabilitymental-healthopen-source

Zombie Dependencies

100/100

Outdated or abandoned libraries that are still widely used, often without active maintenance or clear alternatives.

😂 "Like relying on a haunted vending machine — it still dispenses snacks, but nobody knows how or why it’s still running."

dependenciesmaintenancetechnical-debtrisk

Code Hoarding

100/100

The tendency to accumulate large amounts of code, libraries, or tools—often unused—under the belief they might be useful someday.

😂 "Like stocking up on canned beans for a zombie apocalypse that never comes—except the beans are Python scripts and the zombies are edge cases."

developer-habitstoolingoverengineeringFOMO

License Proliferation

100/100

The overabundance of open source licenses, often with subtle but significant differences, making it difficult to combine or reuse code across projects.

😂 "Like trying to host a potluck where everyone insists on bringing food—but only if you agree to eat it under their very specific house rules."

open-sourcelegalcompatibilitycommunity

Dependency Hell

100/100

A situation where software dependencies become so tangled, outdated, or incompatible that progress grinds to a halt

😂 "Like a soap opera for code: everyone’s connected, nobody’s happy, and you’re stuck watching the drama unfold while your build fails."

package-managementfrustrationsoftware-maintenance
